Gap Analysis: Where Structure is Missing

Five critical gaps have been identified. These are systemic gaps (missing frameworks), not individual failures.

Gap 1: Role Clarity High Impact

Current State: Unclear boundaries, "squishy" overlaps, ambiguous decision authority

Impact: Duplication, gaps in coverage, confusion on priorities, role ambiguity stress

Needed: RACI framework, decision authority levels, explicit role interfaces

Gap 2: Decision Framework High Impact

Current State: Ad-hoc decisions, unclear consultation requirements, no priority criteria

Impact: Decisions revisited, uncertainty about autonomy, stepping on responsibilities

Needed: Decision authority matrix, priority framework, escalation process

Gap 3: Communication Medium Impact

Current State: Ad-hoc patterns, unclear response expectations, informal handovers

Impact: Information gaps, response time frustration, duplication

Needed: Channel map, response time agreements, regular touchpoints

Gap 4: Support Structures High Impact

Current State: No formal mutual support, unclear how to ask for help, limited coordination

Impact: Struggling alone, workload imbalances, missed collaboration, burnout risk

Needed: Workload check-ins, help protocols, coordination mechanisms

Gap 5: Ways of Working High Impact

Current State: Implicit expectations, no boundary agreements, ad-hoc conflict management

Impact: Unsustainable patterns, boundary violations, stress, turnover

Needed: Hours agreements, sustainability commitments, user protocols

Important Note: These are systemic gaps, not failures of individuals. Small teams often operate informally when starting up, but as work intensifies and complexity grows, formal structure becomes essential.

What is a Team Charter?

A Team Charter is a living document created collaboratively by the team that explicitly defines how we work together.

Team-Created

We develop this together, not imposed from outside. Everyone has equal voice.

Evidence-Based

Grounded in our actual work and challenges, not generic advice.

Specific

Concrete agreements, not vague aspirations like "communicate better".

Living Document

Reviewed and adapted as we learn what works and what doesn't.

Closing Thoughts

The team has achieved remarkable things in challenging circumstances. The significant performance growth demonstrates capability and commitment.

The gaps identified in this presentation are normal for a growing organisation. Many teams operate informally in startup phase, but as work intensifies, formal structure becomes essential for sustainability.

This is an opportunity to establish the operational foundations that will enable us to continue delivering excellent work whilst protecting team wellbeing and creating sustainable practices.

This framework has been developed to support team effectiveness and sustainable performance. The approach offers facilitated collaborative process for working through these challenges together.

The question is not whether structure is needed, but whether the team is willing to co-create it together.
